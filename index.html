The building called 325 Kent

One of the most visually dramatic structures to rise lately in New York City is 325 Kent, with its square-donut silhouette and copper and zinc cladding. The residential rental building is the first to open on the Domino Sugar refinery site, a multibillion-dollar, 11-acre, mixed-use community in Williamsburg being developed by Dumbo-based Two Trees Management.

Hearst Tower, New York City

the amazing thing about the Hearst Tower on West 57th Street is that it’s most beautiful up close. The distinctive triangular panels from which architect Norman Foster formed the façade are highly efficient, using 20 percent less steel than more conventional buildings, but that’s almost irrelevant.

Toho Gakuen School of Music

This virtuoso piece of architecture has an almost village-like quality with its independent teaching spaces, clever accouustic treatments and neat communal spaces. It adroitly allows for flexibility, adaptation and improvisation by its students whilst retaining an order and formality.
